Data Analysis and Insights into Maternal Health Outcomes:
A Multifaceted Approach

This project analyzed maternal health data to identify evidence-based actions for improving outcomes during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period. It involves exploratory data analysis, a linear model to predict Systolic Blood Pressure (BP), using selected exploratory variables. Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was applied to streamline the analysis by dimensionality reduction. Additionally, the relationship between age and heart rate was explored through age grouping. Associations between different blood pressure levels were investigated using association mining and clusters of patients with similar Systolic BP were identified to understand patterns and potential risk factors.
